## Emergency Response Protocol for Critical Reef Events

### Activation Criteria
This protocol is activated when ANY of the following conditions are met:
1. AI classification returns "Dead" with confidence > 70%.
2. AI classification returns "Bleached" AND SSTA > 1.5°C AND TSA > 8.
3. Fusion engine returns "Critical" risk level.
4. Field observer reports mass bleaching affecting >50% of surveyed colonies.

### Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0–2 hours)
1. **Alert Dispatch**: CoralGuard automatically sends webhook alerts to configured channels (Slack, email, SMS). Verify alerts were received.
2. **Authority Notification**: Call the marine park authority emergency hotline. Do not rely solely on email during emergencies.
3. **Zone Closure Request**: Submit a formal request to close the affected reef zone to public access. Reference the AI analysis ID and confidence scores.
4. **Team Mobilization**: Contact the nearest rapid-response dive team. Provide them with GPS coordinates and the CoralGuard analysis report.

### Phase 2: Field Response (2–24 hours)
1. **Ground Truth Survey**: Deploy divers to confirm AI classification with in-person assessment. Record species-level data.
2. **Fragment Collection**: Collect fragments from surviving colonies for ex-situ preservation. Target genetically diverse samples.
3. **Environmental Sampling**: Collect water samples for laboratory analysis (nutrients, pollutants, pathogen screening).
4. **Expand Survey Radius**: Check adjacent reef areas within 5km to determine event extent.

### Phase 3: Sustained Response (24 hours – 2 weeks)
1. **Daily Monitoring**: Survey the affected area every 24 hours. Track mortality progression.
2. **Shade Deployment**: If approved, install temporary shade structures over high-value colonies.
3. **Water Quality Management**: Coordinate with upstream land managers to minimize runoff.
4. **Media & Public Communication**: Work with the marine authority's communications team. Issue factual updates based on survey data.
5. **Scientific Reporting**: Begin compiling data for the post-event incident report.

### Phase 4: Recovery Assessment (2 weeks – 6 months)
1. **Recovery Tracking**: Monitor bleached colonies for signs of zooxanthellae return (color recovery).
2. **Mortality Mapping**: Create detailed maps of mortality by species and location.
3. **Resilience Identification**: Flag colonies that survived severe stress as potential "super corals" for future restoration.
4. **Restoration Planning**: Based on mortality data, develop a restoration plan including coral gardening, larval enhancement, or transplantation from nursery stocks.
5. **Lessons Learned**: Conduct a post-event review. Update CoralGuard SOPs and thresholds based on findings.
