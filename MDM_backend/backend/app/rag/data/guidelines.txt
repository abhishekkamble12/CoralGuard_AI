## Standard Operating Procedure: Coral Bleaching Response

### 1. Risk Levels and Thresholds
- **Low Risk**: Sea Surface Temperature Anomaly (SSTA) is below 0.5°C. No active bleaching observed. No immediate action required.
- **Elevated Risk**: SSTA is between 0.5°C and 1.0°C. Early signs of paling or localized bleaching. Action: Increase monitoring frequency and notify local marine park rangers.
- **Critical Risk**: SSTA exceeds 1.0°C and Thermal Stress Anomaly (TSA) is high. Widespread bleaching and potential mortality. Action: Immediately dispatch rapid response field teams. Implement temporary fishing/tourism closures in affected clusters.

### 2. Environmental Clusters
- **Cluster 0**: Shallow water reefs, highly susceptible to rapid thermal stress.
- **Cluster 1**: Deep water reefs, generally resilient but vulnerable to prolonged warming.
- **Cluster -1 (Noise/Anomaly)**: Unusual environmental conditions requiring manual verification.

### 3. Mitigation Strategies
- For localized bleaching, consider deploying localized shading structures if feasible.
- For widespread critical events, focus on identifying resilient "super corals" for future restoration efforts.
